PREVENTIONS

Discover a comprehensive skin cancer prevention guide to shield your skin from harm and preserve its natural glow.

Avoid Direct Sun Exposure

Whenever possible, avoid direct exposure to the sun, especially during peak hours. Use umbrellas or canopies for additional shade.

Sun Protection

Protect your skin from harmful UV rays by wearing sunscreen with SPF 30 or higher, even on cloudy days. Wear protective clothing, such as hats and sunglasses, and seek shade during peak sun hours.

Wear Protective Clothing

Cover up with long-sleeved shirts, long pants, wide-brimmed hats, and sunglasses that provide UV protection. Dark, tightly woven fabrics offer the best sun protection.

Annual Skin Check-ups

Schedule annual skin check-ups with a dermatologist for a thorough examination of your skin to detect any early signs of skin cancer.
