Visualize skin conditions with insightful descriptions.

Basal cell Carcinoma

Basal cell carcinoma is a common, non-melanoma skin cancer, often linked to sun exposure. Early detection is crucial for successful treatment.

Melanoma

Melanoma is a highly aggressive form of skin cancer that develops in the cells (melanocytes) responsible for producing skin pigment.

Melanocytic nevi

Melanocytic nevi, commonly known as moles, are benign skin growths that result from the accumulation of melanocytes. It can vary in size, shape, and color, and they often appear as dark brown or black spots on the skin's surface.

Benign keratosis lesions

Benign keratosis-like lesions are non-cancerous skin growths characterized by rough, scaly patches or bumps on the skin's surface, and may exhibit various colors, ranging from light tan to dark brown.

Actinic Keratosis

Actinic keratoses are pre-cancerous skin growths resulting from prolonged UV radiation exposure, presenting as rough, scaly patches primarily on sun-exposed areas like the face, ears, scalp, neck, hands, and forearms.

Vascular lesions

Vascular lesions are skin abnormalities that involve blood vessels. These lesions can manifest in various forms, ranging from red or purple discolorations to raised or flat growths on the skin's surface.

Dermatofibroma

Dermatofibroma is a small, firm, reddish-brown benign skin tumor commonl y found on the leg s but can occur on any part of the body.
